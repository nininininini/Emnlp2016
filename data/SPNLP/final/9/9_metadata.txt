Abstract#==#We introduce DRAIL, a new declarative framework for specifying Deep Relational
Models. Our framework separates structural considerations, which express domain
knowledge, from the learning architecture to simplify the process of building
complex structural models. 

We show the DRAIL formulation of two NLP tasks, Twitter Part-of-Speech tagging
and Entity-Relation extraction. We compare the performance of different deep
learning architectures for these structural learning tasks.
